Slab buckling

A cold mantle is sinking in an adiabatic mantle. the jump of viscosity at 660 km depth causes the slab to buckle on itself. Video shows the compositional phases and strain rate.

Flat subduction

The oceanic plate subducts with a low angle causing the flat subduction. When the slab steepens back, the stress is accomodated by the deformation of overriding plate. Flat subduction is a highly debated process that could have contributed to the formation of the Andes and Laramide.

Subduction & surface processes

Surface processes are important at convergent boundaries. Sediments are eroded and transported to the trench, which lubricate the subduction interface. Additionally, erosion changes the topographic stress and have consequences on the asymmetric development of the deformation and the topography, forming low angle thrust faults in the Foreland. The video shows 40 Ma of topographic evolution up to the development of a plateau style orogenesis similar to the Central Andes using the ASPECT - FastScape coupling.

Subduction dynamics and continent deformation

Subduction dynamics is key to understand the overall picture behind the formation of subduction type orogenesis (e.g., the Andean orogeny). This video shows how deep processes (flat subduction, buckling) of the oceanic plate relate to important deformation phases of the overriding plate.
